# Character Sheet

## Player

- **Name**: Name of the character
- **Concept**: Usually adjective + noun that describe your character.
- **Race**: Character race (see below) and gender
- **Player**: Name of the player
- **Fellowship**: Name of the Fellowship if any
- **Chronicle**: Title of the campaign if any

## Attributes

### Mental Attributes

- **Intelligence**: The raw power of the mind. Cognitive capacity. The inherent capability to digest, comprehend and remember information and to learn more. Intelligence is a direct measure of how smart your character is.
- **Wits**: The ability to think, under pressure or duress, without letting them see you sweat. Wits also encompasses an eye for detail, the ability to absorb what’s going on in the environment, and to react to events. Wits involves the powers of perception and response.
- **Resolve**: The focus and determination. The capacity to stay on target, ignore distractions and to resist coercion or browbeating. Resolve is your character’s mental fortitude. His personal conviction. His clarity of vision or spirit.

### Physical Attributes

- **Strength**: Physical might. Sheer bodily power. The capacity to lift objects, move items, hit things and people, and do damage. Strength is a measure of muscle.
- **Dexterity**: Quickness. Response time. A delicate touch. Dexterity indicates how quickly and with how much finesse your character responds to his physical world. Dexterity dots help you to react to trouble. Dexterity also helps with hand-eye coordination, like to fire an accurate shot or to juggle objects
- **Stamina**: Sturdiness. Steadfastness. Sheer physical resilience. Stamina is a measure of how tough your character is. It indicates how far she can push her body, and how much physical abuse she can endure.

### Social Attributes

- **Presence**: Bearing. Stature. Assertiveness. Presence suggests the power of your character’s very identity. Attractiveness is only part of the trait. It reflects her sheer command over the attention of others. It’s her capacity to impose her will on others.
- **Manipulation**: Charm. Persuasiveness. Charisma. The capacity to play upon the desires, hopes and needs of others to influence them. Manipulation reflects how well he can appeal to, gain the favor of and generally coerce others.
- **Composure**: Poise. Dignity. The capacity to remain calm and unfazed in social and threatening situations, usually harrowing ones. This trait is a measure of emotional fortitude, restraint and calm.

## Skills

### Mental Skills

- **Academic**: Education and general knowledge in the Arts and Humanities, everything from English to history, economics to law.
Specialties: Anthropology, Art, English, History, Law, Religion…
- **Craft**: Creating works of physical art or construction with his hands.
Specialties: Forging, Painting, Sculpting, Sewing, Wood crafting…
- **Investigation**: Art of solving mysteries, examining seemingly disparate evidence to find a connection, answering riddles and overcoming paradoxes.
Specialties: Artifacts, Crime Scenes, Cryptography, Dreams, Riddles…
- **Mechanisms**: Understand and create machineries and mechanisms.
Specialties: Design, Fixing, Traps, Puzzle…
- **Medicine**: Expertise in how to treat injuries and illness. The trait represents knowledge of human anatomy and basic medical treatments.
Specialties: Emergency Care, Pathology, Pharmaceuticals, Surgery…
- **Occult**: Knowledge of legends and the supernatural. She knows the theories, myths and legends of the occult, and can also discern fact from rumor.
Specialties: Cultural Beliefs, Ghosts, Magic, Monsters, Superstitions, Witchcraft
- **Politics**: To know how the political process works, who to contact to get something done. Who is in power and how she got there, along with her potential rivals.
Specialties: Bribery, Elections, Federal, Local, State, Scandals…
- **Science**: Physical and natural sciences: biology, chemistry, geology, meteorology, physics.
Specialties: Biology, Chemistry, Geology, Metallurgy, Physics…

### Physical Skills

- **Athletics**: Physical training applied to any action that requires prolonged physical exertion or that demands considerable hand-eye coordination.
Specialties: Climbing, Long-Distance Running, Sprinting, Swimming, Throwing
- **Brawl**: Unarmed combat, know how to hit an opponent and how to defend themselves from attack. Using fists, elbows, knees, shoulders…
Specialties: Blocking, Boxing, Grappling, Kung Fu, Throws…
- **Drive**: Operate a transportation means (horse, coach, boat…) under difficult or dangerous conditions. Skill is not needed in normal conditions.
Specialties: Horse, Boar, Coach, Pursuit…
- **Larceny**: Larceny is a broad Skill that covers everything from picking locks to concealing stolen goods and everything in between.
Specialties: Lock picking, Pickpocketing, Security Systems, Safecracking
- **Shoot**: Identify, operate and maintain most types of projectile weapons like bows and crossbows.
Specialties: Long Bow, Short Bow, Crossbow…
- **Stealth**: Experience in avoiding notice, whether by moving silently, making use of cover or blending into a crowd.
Specialties: Camouflage, Crowds, Moving in Darkness, Moving in Woods…
- **Survival**: Training in living off the land. The character knows where to find food and shelter, and how to endure harsh environmental conditions.
Specialties: Foraging, Navigation, Meteorology, Shelter…
- **Weaponry**: Training in fighting with a melee weapon like a knife or an axe. 
Specialties: Improvised Weapons, Knives, Swords…

### Social Skills

- **Animal Ken**: Interpret and recognize the behavior of animals is something else entirely. Your character intuitively grasps or has been trained to read animals to know how they react to situations.
Specialties: Animal Needs, Imminent Attack, Specific Kind of Animal, Training…
- **Empathy**: Intuition for reading people’s emotions. Capacity to understand other people’s views and perspectives, whether your character agrees with those positions or not.
Specialties: Emotion, Lies, Motives, Personalities
- **Expression**: Training in the art of communication, both to entertain and inform. This Skill covers both the written and spoken word but also dance.
Specialties: Classical Dance, Musical Instrument, Speeches…
- **Intimidation**: Technique of persuading others through the use of fear. That can be a show of brute or verbal threats or menacing body language.
Specialties: Bluster, Physical Threats, Stare-Downs, Torture, Veiled Threats
- **Persuasion**: Inspiring or changing minds through logic, charm or sheer, glib fast-talking. Convincing others by force of personality alone.
Specialties: Inspiring Troops, Motivational Speeches, Sales Pitches, Seduction…
- **Socialize**: Ability to interact with others in a variety of situations, from talking people up at bars to comporting himself with dignity at official dinners.
Specialties: Bar Hopping, Dress Balls, Formal Events, Frat Parties.
- **Streetwise**: Character knows how life on the streets works and is adept at surviving by its harsh rules. gather information, make contacts, buy and sell on the black market, and otherwise make use of the street’s unique resources
Specialties: Black Market, Gangs, Rumors, Undercover Operations
- **Subterfuge**: The characters know how to lie convincingly, and recognize when being lied to. How to hide one’s emotions or reactions, and to avoid being tricked.
Specialties: Hiding Emotions, Lying, Misdirection, Spotting Lies…

## Others Traits

### Merits

Special capabilities or knacks that add individuality to your character. They are purchased during character creation or with experience points.

### Energies

- **Health**: Capacity to cope with injury and remain functional. Each point of damage inflicted lowers his Health. When only 3 points of health remain, the character suffers a -1 dice penalty. This penalty increases to -2 and -3 as health diminishes. A character with 0 health left, is knocked out.
- **Willpower**: self-confidence, determination and emotional resilience. 
Points are spent representing the character putting all into one action and trying to reach his heroic potential. He gains +3 dice for the next action.
- **Mastery**: It represents the character’s potency to use magic, his connection to the primal forces and his capacity to work their energies.
- **Mana**: The fluid of magic, supernal energy in its pure form. Mages use mana as fuel for casting spells. The source of mana and the shape the mage gives to it depends on his path.

### Advantages

- **XP**: At the end of each scenario, players earn experience points (XP) that can be spent to increase character traits.
- **Size**: height of the character, depending on its race.
- **Speed**: number of yards a character can move in one turn.
- **Defense**: an active defense, dodge and movements. Malus for attackers.
- **Armor**: a passive defense, gears. Malus for attackers.
- **Initiative**: Scores that determine the order in which characters act in a turn.

### Paths
The Magic is divided in 7 Paths that work in tandem, Air is opposed to Earth, Fire is opposed to Water, Evil is opposed to Holy and Mind is alone.

- **Air**: Control the air. 1 dot: Control the blow, 2 dots: Lift a cup, 3 dots: Lift a chair, 4 dots: Lift a table, 5 dots Fly
- **Earth**: Control ground. 1 dot: Control dust, 2 dots: Move a stone, 3 dots: Move a rock, 4 dots: Stone wall, 5 dots: Earth quake
- **Fire**: Control fire. 1 dot: Control fire, 2 dots: Create candle flame (1L), 3 dots: Create torch flame (2L), 4 dots: Create camp fire (3L), 5 dots: Immune to fire
- **Water**: Control water. 1 dot: Control water, 2 dots: Create cup of water, 3 dots: Create bucket of water, 4 dots: Create wave, 5 dots: Breath under water
- **Evil**: Control the darkness. 1 dot: Control shadows, 2 dots: Call the ghosts, 3 dots: Leech life (1H), 4 dots: Devil shape, 5 dots: Invocation
- **Holy**: Control the light. 1 dot: Heal 1B, 2 dots: Heal 1L to 1B, 3 dots: Heal 1L, 4 dots: Heal 1A to 1L, 5 dots: Heal 1L
- **Mind**: Mind control. 1 dot: Suggestion, 2 dots: Telepathy, 3 dots: Read Mind, 4 dots: Give Order, 5 dots: Possession

### Equipment

- **Weapon**: List and characteristics of weapons.
- **Equipment**: List and characteristics of shields and armors.
